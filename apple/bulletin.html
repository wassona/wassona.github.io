Develop A Wellness Policy

A policy is a standard that your program and your staff commit to. Developing policies can be simple and can be done a little bit at a time.

  • A clear, written Wellness Policy demonstrates your commitment to improving the health of the children in your care.
  • Policies help families and staff understand the importance of physical activity and healthy eating for young children.
  • A sample wellness policy for your program is provided each time you complete a Healthy Apple Assessment.
  • Click here for help thinking through what information to include on your Wellness Policy.

Partner with Families

Parents/guardians are committed to their children's well-being, and families can reap the rewards of being active together and developing healthy habits. Sharing how easy and important it is to be active with their kids provides encouragement and motivation.

  • Communicate with and involve families — there are many ways to do this, from newsletters and policy handouts to family events to text messages, social media postings and more.
  • Check Chapter 6 of the Healthy Apple Physical Activity Toolkit for more tips on engaging families.

Your Healthy Apple Score:
Measuring Progress & Celebrating Achievements

Take the Healthy Apple Physical Activity and Nutrition Assessments to get your Healthy Apple Score. By knowing your starting point, you can measure progress and recognize your successes.

  • Your score tells you which child care best practices you are achieving and which need some improvement.
  • Complete the assessments as many times as you like to see how you are improving and to take the opportunity to celebrate your achievements.

The Healthy Apple Assessments are free, convenient, and easy to use.

Sample Wellness Policy

A policy is a standard that your program and your staff commit to. Having a clear written policy is important for showing your commitment to health, but it can be hard to know where to start.

  • Click here to see a Sample Wellness Policy — borrow from it and adapt it for your program.
  • A sample wellness policy for your program is provided each time you complete a Healthy Apple Assessment.
  • Build your Wellness Policy in steps. Document practices that are already in place. Then add those you can easily adopt or commit to improving.

Taking the Healthy Apple Assessment can help you prioritize what to focus on first and next.

Making It Stick

Making long-lasting changes to your child care program is like making any kind of change. Key ingredients or supports can make it much easier to make and sustain changes.

Learn about some key ingredients for lasting changes by hovering over each bulletin board item or click the pulsing dots. Each can be accomplished one step at a time with little effort and each results in big benefits.
